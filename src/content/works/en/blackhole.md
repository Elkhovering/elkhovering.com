## Context

Blackhole was one of my earliest commercial projects — a combined Bitcoin wallet and on-chain roulette game, built when blockchain entertainment was still finding its visual language. The brief: space. Everything else was open.

## Problem

Two completely different product surfaces — a utility wallet requiring trust and clarity, and a roulette game requiring energy and excitement — had to share a single visual world without the wallet feeling like a casino or the casino feeling like a bank.

## My role

Art direction, design, and full frontend. First project where I had full creative ownership from concept to launch.

## Process

Built the space world as the unifying frame — both products exist in the same "Blackhole" universe. The wallet UI was clean and geometric (mission control aesthetic); the roulette was atmospheric and glowing (deep space aesthetic). The Canvas-based roulette animation was the technical challenge: smooth rotation, physics feel, winning moment.

## Outcome

A formative project — early career signal piece that proved I could handle both utility product design and entertainment design simultaneously. Set the tone for all dark/editorial work that followed.
