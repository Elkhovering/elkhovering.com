## Context

NUEF is a music label with a deliberate, slow aesthetic — somewhere between ambient and post-industrial. The site needed to feel like the music: immersive without being loud, editorial without being cold.

## Problem

Most music label sites are either over-designed portfolio dumps or plain playlist embeds. NUEF wanted something with atmosphere — a site that functions as an extension of the label's sonic identity.

## My role

Art direction and full frontend. I defined the visual language, built the transition system, and shipped the site without a design handoff — all decisions happened in the browser.

## Process

I built the animation system around the idea of "scene transitions" — each page section enters and exits like a film cut, not a scroll. Heavy use of GSAP timelines, careful typography pacing, and a controlled color palette (near-black, off-white, single accent).

## Outcome

The site attracted significant attention outside the label's existing audience — designers and developers shared it as a reference. It pulled in several artist inquiries that turned into signed projects.
