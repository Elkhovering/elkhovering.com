## Context

The project came in through word of mouth. By 2020 I was already moving in the crypto community, mostly as a designer, and the brief was the classic one — *«we need visuals that hit. Not like everyone else's.»*

The landing had two jobs: pull attention onto the token so it could build economic mass, and pre-announce a casino running on that same token. No brief, full trust, a blank canvas.

## Concept

I pulled together everything living in the overlap of bananas and crypto and started riffing. The banana-katana came almost by accident — I needed an arresting hero opener, something that would carve a path into the acidic violet jungle below. No sacred meaning there. Pure impact.

## Process

Two weeks, end to end. Not enough room for anything slow — the structure locked in on AIDA (attention → interest → desire → action), and content, animations, transitions all got planned off that skeleton.

A manager held the client loop. Design and frontend — my hands on both.

## Craft

Everything on the page got layered into parallax depths. Leaves at the edges are intentionally blurred and clipped — they sit as the closest plane, reinforcing the sense that you're looking through foliage into the scene.

## Outcome

Client accepted it on the first pass — no revisions. His words: *«you can really go into space with visuals.»* After launch, token holders pushed the landing themselves — it became part of how the community talked about the project.

Looking at it today, I'd tighten the typography and redo a couple of buttons. For something shipped in two weeks four years ago, it aged well.
