## Context

Syrnik.dev is my indie game studio — a side project running parallel to my client work, focused on small experimental games at the intersection of economy, strategy, and systems design. The site needed to communicate personality before product.

## Problem

Most indie studio sites are either dev blogs or Steam page mirrors. I wanted something that made you feel the studio's sensibility the moment you landed — playful, slightly absurd, unmistakably mine.

## My role

Everything. Founder, art director, designer, frontend developer. Built in spare hours across several weeks.

## Process

The mascot — a cheesecake character — came first, before a single page was designed. Once the mascot was right, the visual language followed naturally. Built the site in Astro with Three.js for the 3D elements, GSAP for animation. The design is deliberately light and soft, which creates contrast with my darker client work.

## Outcome

The site has a growing community of followers — developers and players who found it through shares on social and game dev communities. Several collaborators reached out after seeing the site. Ongoing: the site grows as the studio ships more games.
