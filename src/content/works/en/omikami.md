## Context

Omikami is a token project with a visual identity rooted in Japanese mythology — kami spirits, sacred geometry, the tension between ancient and digital. The launch landing needed to live up to that premise.

## Problem

Most token launches use the same Spline template or low-effort Three.js sphere. Omikami needed a scene that felt genuinely crafted — 3D that justified its own presence on the page, not 3D as decoration.

## My role

Art direction, 3D scene design, shader development, and full frontend. I owned the visual concept from first sketches through launch.

## Process

Designed the 3D scene around a central sacred geometry object — dynamic shaders responding to scroll and mouse, with a particle system evoking the atmospheric feel of Shinto aesthetics. Built GLSL shaders for the surface material. Performance passes to keep it smooth on mid-range mobile.

## Outcome

The landing generated strong community reaction pre-launch — screenshots circulated across crypto Twitter and Telegram. It was cited by the project team as the primary driver of early community growth ahead of the token launch date.
